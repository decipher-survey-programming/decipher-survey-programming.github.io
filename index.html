Q. Who are the Github organization owners?

A. Brad and hopefully Shaen and Jeremy. We can consider adding more, but I'd prefer to keep the group small.

Q. Who can contribute?

A. Anyone via pull requests

Q. Can we create new repos?

A. Yes

Q. Can we organize the content differently?

A. Yes, but let's decide on a structure and then stick with it. Once decided, we'll share with the other programmers.

Q. Why have this at all?

A. Because there are a lot of programmer tools floating around but poor visibility and no central repository

Q. Who has access to this content?

A. It's public, so be smart about what you put in here

Q. Who is liable if something bad happens as a result of using this stuff?

A. According to the MIT License, the user. Still, we should carefully review anything that gets contributed

Q. How do I use stuff that's contributed by others?

A. My current approach is to clone the repo to a subdirectory under my $HOME on Ares, then link to the scripts I want to use in my $HOME/bin
